The Fundamentals

Core Web3 Concepts

Four side-by-side comparisons that capture the mental model shifts behind decentralized tech.

Web2 vs Web3

The shift from platforms to protocols

WEB2 WEB3
Who owns data The platform (Meta, Google) You – in your wallet
Identity Email + password per app One wallet, every app
Money Banks, Stripe, PayPal Native to the protocol
Trust model Trust the company Trust the code & math
Backend Centralized servers Smart contracts on a chain

Ethereum vs Bitcoin

Two very different visions for a blockchain

BITCOIN ETHEREUM
Purpose Digital gold, store of value Programmable world computer
Smart contracts Very limited scripting Full Turing-complete (Solidity)
Consensus Proof of Work Proof of Stake (since 2022)
Block Time ~10 minutes ~12 seconds
Supply Capped at 21M BTC No hard cap, deflationary

Public Key vs Private Key

The two halves of your crypto identity

PUBLIC KEY PRIVATE KEY
Share it? Yes – it's your address Never. Ever. Not once.
What it does Receives funds & messages Signs transactions
If lost... No problem, derive again All funds gone forever
Analogy Your bank account number Your master password + PIN
Length 20 bytes (Ethereum address) 32 bytes (256-bit secret)

Blockchain vs Traditional Database

Why a chain isn't just a slow Postgres

TRADITIONAL DB BLOCKCHAIN
Who can write Admin with credentials Anyone with a valid signature
Can data be edited Yes – UPDATE/DELETE freely No – append-only, immutable
Who verifies Trust the DB owner Thousands of independent nodes
Downtime Server can go down Network keeps running 24/7
Best for Internal app data, speed Money, ownership, trustless tracking
